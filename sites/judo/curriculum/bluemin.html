Blue Belt Curriculum

Minor / Juvenile


Judo history

    Who are some famous Olympic judoka?
    Who are some famous Olympic judo coaches?
What I need to know about refereeing a tournament.

    You have to referee at three matches in you club?
    In newaza, when do you bring the competitors back to their feet?
    Do you always accept the referee's decision?
Throwing moves I have to learn.

    Yoko tomoe nage (side stomach throw)
    Harai tsuri komi ashi (sweeping ankle throw)
    Hane maki komi (springing sacrifice throw; you sacrifice your own balance)
    Uchimata sukashi (counter to uchimata)
    O guruma (Major wheel throw)
    Yoko guruma (Side wheeling throw)
    Tewaza gaeshi (flour bag throw)
    Uchi makikomi (Inner winding throw)
    O soto makikomi (Outside rolling and reaping throw)
Neck locks I have to learn.

    Katate jime (single hand neck-lock)
    Ryote jime (two hand neck-lock)
    Sode guruma jime (Sleeve neck-lock)
What are the principles of judo I must know.

    Nage no kata (Forms of throwing, Set number 2)
      Uki goshi (floating hip throw)
      Harai goshi (Sweeping leg throw, remember to keep the sweeping leg straight)
      Tsuri komi goshi (lift, propping hip throw)
What arm-lock techniques I have to know.

    Ude hishigi waki gatame (arm-bar under your armpit)
    Ude hishigi hara gatame (arm-bar using your stomach)
    Ude hishigi ashi gatame (arm-bar using your leg)
    Ude hishigi te gatame (arm-bar using your hand and arm as a lever)
    Ude hishigi sankaku gatame (arm-bar using your leg as a lever from the sankaku jime position)
How to fall so I don't hurt myself.

    Be able to fall from all throws at this level.
    Be better at all break-falls, than you were before.
