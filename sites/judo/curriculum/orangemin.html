Orange Belt Curriculum

Minor / Juvenile


What I need to know about a tournament.

    A competition area is a big red square with green mats in the center.
    There are 2 judges and one referee.
    You must bow at the edge of the mat, then walk to the Blue or white line, bow again and step over the line and wait for the referee to call hajime.
    You can win by an ippon in a throw on in a hold-down.
    You have to do everything the referee tells you to.
Throwing moves I have to learn.

    Tsuri komi goshi (lift your partners collar forward and up and throw them)
    Tsuri goshi (lift your partners belt from behind, forward and up and throw them)
    Sode tsuri komi goshi (lift your partners both sleeves, forward and up and throw them)
    Ushiro goshi (rear lifting hip throw)
    Sasae tsuri komi ashi (blocking ankle throw)
    O uchi gaeshi (counter to O uchi gari)
    Ko uchi gaeshi (counter to Ko uchi gari)
    Tani otoshi (rear dropping throw)
Holding moves I have to learn.

    Mune gatame (hold down the chest by one shounder)
    Tate shiho gatame (hold down the chest by getting on top of your partner)
How to hold my partners uniform so they won't get away.

    Kenka yotsu (reverse grip)
    Back grip
    Know the competition rules for grip
How to fall so I don't hurt myself.

    Be able to fall from all throws at this level
    Falls over a horse position
    Zempo kaiten or forward roll from the standing position
    Koho kaiten or backward roll from the standing position
    Combination forward and backward roll.
Words I have to know.

    Sono mama = do not move
    Yoshi = continue
    Osae komi = holdown in progress
    Toketa = hold is broken
    Waza ari = a score of 7 or almost win is awarded
    Yuko = a score of 5 or half point is awarded
    Koka = a score of 3 or a minor score is awarded
    Keikoku = a penalty of 7 is awarded against you
    Chui = a penalty of 5 is awarded against you
    Shido = a penalty of 3 is awarded against you
    Hansoku make = you lose the match by a penalty
    Hantai = the referee calls the judges to decide who wins after a tie.
