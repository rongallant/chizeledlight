Brown Belt Curriculum

Junior / Senior


Throwing Techniques (Tachi waza)

    Yoko gake – side hooking throw
    Yoko otoshi – side dropping throw
    Tani otoshi – valley drop throw
    Utsuri goshi – switching hip throw
    guruma – big wheel throw
    HaraI makikomi – Sweeping loin sacrifice throw
    Yoko wakare – side separation throw
    Sumi otoshi – corner drop throw
    Sukui nage – scooping throw
    Ashi guruma – leg wheel

Grappling Techniques (Holding) (Katame waza)

    Kata osae gatame – shoulder locking hold
    Mune gatame – cross chest hold
    Tate shiho gatame – straight-locking four-corner hold

Shime waza (Strangulation techniques)

    Katate jime – one-hand choke
    Ryote jime – two-hand choke
    Sode guruma jime – sleeve wheel choke
    Tsuri komi jime – thrust choke

Kansetsu waza (Arm-lock techniques)

    Ude hishigi hara gatame – stomach armlock
    Ude hishigi ashi gatame – leg armlock
    Ude hishigi te gatame – hand armlock
    Ude hishigi sankaku gatame – triangular armlock using the legs

Nage no kata (Kata of throwing techniques)

    Okuri ashi harai – double foot sweep
    Sasae tsuri komi ashi – propping drawing ankle throw
    Uchimata – inner thigh throw

Important: The formality of the kata is not emphasized at this level. What the student should practise is the action/reaction principle of the movement. It is most important that the student commit to a full attack and a follow through (keeping in mind that safety for the partner is most important). The tori (thrower) must react and harmonize to this committed attack and execute his/her own throw. If there is no full attack, there can be no throw. The student will then have to rely on muscle strength. This is not judo.

Katame no kata (Kata of grappling techniques)

    Ude garami –entangled arm-lock
    Ude hishigi juji gatame – cross arm-lock
    Ude hishigi ude gatame – arm-bar lock
    Ude hishigi hiza gatame – arm-lock using the knee
    Ashi garami – entangled leg-lock

Important – The purpose of this kata [form] is to develop good basic skills in groundwork. Each movement has three attempted escapes and three follow up attacks to these attempts. The student has to feel the movement and instead of using muscle to resist the choke, s/he should follow the action of the partner to neutralize or escape the choke.

Note - The formal bowing and sliding movement on the knees is not important at this level.

Renraku waza (Combination techniques in standing judo)

    soto gari into morote seoinage
    Uchimata into kouchi gari
    uchi gari into tai otoshi

Or

Demonstrate three combination techniques chosen from among the techniques in the Blue Belt curriculum.

 

Kaeshi waza (Counter techniques in standing judo)

    Tomoe nage against ko uchi gari
    Hiza guruma against hane goshi
    Utsuri goshi against hane goshi

Or

Three counter attacks chosen from among the techniques in the Blue Belt curriculum.

Kumi kata

Demonstrate three waza from the Brown Belt curriculum using alternate gripping techniques.
