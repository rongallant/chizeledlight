Blue Belt Curriculum

Junior / Senior


Throwing Techniques (Tachi waza)

    Harai tsuri komi ashi – lift-pull ankle sweep
    Sumi Gaeshi – corner throw
    O soto guruma – major reaping wheel throw
    Ura nage – rear sacrifice throw
    Yama arashi – mountain storm throw
    Soto maki komi – outer wrap-around throw
    Uchimata sukashi – inner reaping thigh counter throw
    Yoko guruma – side wheel sacrifice throw
    Seoi otoshi – shoulder drop throw

Grappling Techniques (Holding) (Katame waza)

    Makura kesa gatame – pillow hold
    Ushiro kesa gatame – rear scarf hold
    Kuzure yoko shiho gatame – modified side four quarter hold

Shime waza (Strangulation techniques)

    Hadaka jime – naked choke using just the hands and arm/wrist
    Sankaku jime – triangle choke using the legs

Kansetsu waza (Arm-lock techniques)

    Ude hishigi ude gatame- armlock using the straight arm
    Ude hishigi hiza gatame – armlock using your knee
    Ude hishigi waki gatame – armlock using your armpit

Nage no kata (Kata of throwing techniques)

    Uki goshi – floating hip throw
    Harai goshi – sweeping loin throw
    Tsuri komi goshi – lift pull throw

Important: The formality of the kata is not emphasized at this level. What the student should practise is the action/reaction principle of the movement. It is most important that the student commit to a full attack and a follow through (keeping in mind that safety for the partner is most important). The tori (thrower) must react and harmonize to this committed attack and execute his/her own throw. If there is no full attack, there can be no throw. The student will then have to rely on muscle strength. This is not judo.

Katame no kata (Kata of grappling techniques)

    Kata juji jime – half-cross choke
    Hadaka jime – naked choke from behind
    Okuri eri jime – sliding collar choke
    Kataha jime –choke from behind by locking the arm
    Gyaku juji jime – reverse cross choke

Important – The purpose of this kata [form] is to develop good basic skills in groundwork. Each movement has three attempted escapes and three follow up attacks to these attempts. The student has to feel the movement and instead of using muscle to resist the choke, s/he should follow the action of the partner to neutralize or escape the choke.

Note - The formal bowing and sliding movement on the knees is not important at this level.

Renraku waza (Combination techniques in standing judo)

    Osoto gari into harai tsuri komi ashi
    Osoto gari into Kosoto gari
    Ko uchi gari into soto makikomi
Or

Demonstrate three combination techniques chosen from among the techniques in the Blue Belt curriculum.

Kaeshi waza (Counter techniques in standing judo)

    Ura nage against harai goshi
    Uchimata sukashi against uchimata
    Yama arashi against kuchiki taoshi

Or

Three counter attacks chosen from among the techniques in the Blue Belt curriculum.

Kumi kata

Demonstrate three techniques from the Blue Belt curriculum using alternate gripping techniques.

Judo Tournament Terminology

    Sogo gachi – a compound win ( your score + your opponents penalty).
    Fusen gachi – to win a contest because your opponent did not show.
    Waza ari awasete ippon – to win by two waza ari’s in a contest
    Hiki wake – a draw in a contest (usually in team competition)
    Hantei – a decision
    Kiken hachi – to win because your opponent has to withdraw.
    Yusei gachi – to win a contest by leading on the scoreboard at the end of the match.
    Maitta – I give up
