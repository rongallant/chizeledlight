Yellow Belt Curriculum

Junior / Senior


Etiquette

    Care for the judo gi (judo uniform)
    Tie the belt (obi)
    Bowing: standing and kneeling positions (Rei)
    Respect and courtesy for senior ranks.

History

    History of judo in Japan
    History of judo in Canada
    History of judo in Newfoundland

Throwing Techniques (Tachi waza)

    O goshi – major hip throw
    Seoinage (Morote) – Two arm shoulder throw
    De ashi barai (harai) – advancing foot sweep
    Uki goshi – floating hip throw
    O soto gari – major outer reaping leg throw
    O soto otoshi – major outer drop throw
    Kuchiki taoshi (daoshi) – one-hand drop throw
    Kibisu gaeshi – Heel trip throw

Grappling Techniques (Holding) (Katame waza).

    Hon kesa gatame and counter - Scarf hold.
    Kata gatame and counter – Shoulder hold.
    Kami shiho gatame and counter –Top four-corner hold.

Break-fall Techniques (Ukemi waza)

    Back break-falls from the: sitting, squatting, and standing positions.
    Forward rolling from the: kneeling, squatting, and standing positions.
    Side break-falls from the: sitting, squatting, and standing positions.

Judo Terminology

    Sensei – judo teacher
    Dojo – practise hall
    Tatami – judo mat
    Rei – to bow
    Randori – free practise
    Shiai – judo contest
    Judogi – judo uniform
    Judoka – Judo player
