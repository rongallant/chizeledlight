Green Belt Curriculum

Minor / Juvenile


Judo history

    How did judo start in Canada?
    Who are some famous Canadian judoka?
What I need to know about refereeing a tournament.

    How does the referee decide what is an Ippon, waza ari, yoko, or koka?
    How does the referee decide what is a Hansoku make, keikoku, chui, or shido?
Throwing moves I have to learn.

    Uchimata (inner thigh throw. Remember to spring on the supporting leg)
    Uchimata gaeshi (counter throw to Uchimata)
    Yama arashi (mountain storm throw)
    Hiza guruma (knee wheel throw)
    Uki waza (floating corner throw)
    Seoi otoshi (shoulder drop throw, using either one or two knees down)
    Ashi guruma (leg wheel throw)
    Kata guruma (shoulder wheel)
Neck locks I have to learn.

    Nami juji jime (normal necklock)
    Gyaky juji jime (reverse necklock, opposite to name juji jime)
    Kata juji jime (combination necklock, combine the hand positions of name juji jime and gyaku juji jime)
What are the principles of judo I must know.

    Nage no kata (Forms of throwing, Set 1)
      Uki otoshi (floating drop throw)
      Ippon seoinage (Performed as the uke attacks from 1.8 meters away)
      Kata guruma (performed as the uke attacks by pushing you)
Gripping Techniques I need to know.

    Three different gripping techniques
    One counter or break to each new gripping technique
How to fall so I don't hurt myself.

    Be able to fall from all throws at this level.
    Be better at all break-falls, than you were before.
