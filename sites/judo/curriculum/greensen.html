Green Belt Curriculum

Junior / Senior


Throwing Techniques (Tachi waza)

    Hiza guruma – knee wheel throw
    Hane goshi – Springing hip throw
    Hane goshi gaeshi – Counter to the springing hip throw
    Tomoe nage –Stomach throw
    Kata guruma – shounder throw
    Uchimata – Inner throw throw
    Ushiro goshi - Rear hip throw
    Okuri ashi barai – Double foot sweep
    Morote gari – Two arm reaping throw
    Uki waza – Floating drop throw

Osaekomi waza (Holding techniques)

    Yoko shiho gatame and counter

Shime waza (Strangulation/choking techniques)

    Okuri eri jime – Sliding collar choke
    Kata ha jime – Broken sliding collar choke

Kansetsu waza (Arm-lock techniques)

    Ude hishigi juji gatame
    Ude garami

Nage no kata (Randori kata on the feet) Set 1

    Uki goshi
    Ippon seoinage
    Kata guruma

Important - The formality of the kata is not emphasized at this level. What the student should practise is the action/reaction principle of the movement. It is most important that the student commit to a full attack and a follow through (keeping in mind that safety for the partner is most important). The tori (thrower) must react and harmonize to this committed attack and execute his/her own throw. If there is no full attack, there can be no throw. The student will then have to rely on muscle strength,and this is not judo.

Katame no kata (Grappling kata) Set 1

    (Kuzure) kesa gatame – Scarf hold
    Kata gatame – Shoulder lock
    Kami shiho gatame – Upper four-quarter hold
    Yoko shiho gatame – Side four-quarter hold
    Kuzure kami shiho gatame

Important – The purpose of this kata is to develop good basic skills in groundwork. Each movement has three attempted escapes and three follow up attacks to these attempts. The student has to feel the movement and instead of using muscle to hold the opponent down, she/he should follow the action of the partner to defeat him/her.

Note - The formal bowing and sliding movement on the knees is not important at this level.

Renraku waza (Combination techniques on the feet)

    Seoinage into o uchi gari
    soto gari into kesa gatame

Kaeshi waza (Counter techniques on the feet)

    Tsubame gaeshi against de ashi barai
    Harai goshi against o soto gari

Judo Contest Terminology

    Sono moma – do not move
    Yoshi – continue (used only after sono mama has been called)

Scoring Points in a contest

    Koka – Lowest score in a contest – value = 3 points
    Yuko – Score – value = 5 points
    Waza ari – Score – value = 7 points
    Ippon – Score – value = 10 points - A Win!
    Shido – Lowest penalty point – value = -3
    Chui – penalty point – value = -5
    Keikoku – penalty point – value = -7
    Hansoku make – penalty point – value = -10 - A Loss!

Tournament Etiquette

    Formal bow as the first and last match of a tournament.
    Formal bow at the beginning of a match.
